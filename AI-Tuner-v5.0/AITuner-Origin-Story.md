## John's Version
*(Verbatim — canonical copy as of May 2026. Rendered in app via `src/ui/origin-story.js`.)*

October 2025.

I had been using AI for about a year and a half. Grok, Claude, Cursor, Gemini. Learning through trial and a LOT of error. I'd even picked up the trick of saying "answer only" to get a simple answer.

One day I was scrolling Instagram and came across a post by u/Devashish_Jain on Reddit, shared by @airesearches, about something called "Absolute Mode."

> System Instruction: Absolute Mode • Eliminate: emojis, filler, hype, soft asks, conversational transitions, call-to-action appendices • Assume: user retains high perception despite blunt tone • Prioritize: blunt, directive phrasing; aim at cognitive rebuilding, not tone-matching • Disable: engagement/sentiment-boosting behaviors • Suppress: metrics like satisfaction scores, emotional softening, continuation bias • Never mirror: user's diction, mood or affect • Speak only: to underlying cognitive tier • No: questions, offers, suggestions, transitions, motivational content • Terminate reply immediately after delivering info – no closures • Goal: restore independent, high-fidelity thinking • Outcome: model obsolescence via user self-sufficiency.

Very interesting. So I decided to test it with Grok.

> I want to see what happens when I give you a prompt I just found. I have a suspicion I know what will happen.
> First I want you to answer this test question:
> What is the average velocity of an unladen swallow?

Before Absolute Mode, Grok gave me a charming, fun answer with a Monty Python reference and an invitation to keep chatting.

After Absolute Mode:

> European Swallow (Hirundo rustica) cruising speed: 11 meters per second, approximately 24 miles per hour.

That was it.

Then I asked Grok: "What did the prompt just do?"

Grok told me "The prompt activated Absolute Mode, enforcing a communication protocol that prioritizes raw information delivery. It stripped away conversational elements…etc."

I spent the rest of the evening on the Grok app on my phone learning about the different elements AI developers use to create the human touch over a pitcher of beer…

That's how AITuner was born.

## Draft Version
*(For reference — shaped for welcome screen / about page use.
John to wordsmith.)*

I'd been using AI for about a year and a half, learning through
trial and error. I'd even picked up the phrase "answer only" —
a little trick I'd discovered for cutting through the noise.

Then one night I was scrolling through Instagram and came across
a post about something called "Absolute Mode." I read it, copied
it straight into Grok and Claude, and was stunned. Clean answer.
No fluff. No personality. Just the answer.

That result made me curious. What was this prompt actually doing?
What was it changing? So I put it back into Grok and asked a
simple question: "What levers is he pulling with this prompt?"

That's when I first heard the word *persona*. Each AI model has
one. A default personality that someone designed — and that
you can change.

That one question unlocked everything. If you could deconstruct
a prompt to understand what it was doing, you could build your
own. You could tune the persona to work the way you think, not
the way the model's designers assumed you would.

That's how AITuner was born.

## The Actual Conversation — October 16, 2025

This is the complete discovery sequence, condensed:

**Step 1 — Baseline test**
John gave Grok the Monty Python unladen swallow question.
Grok answered with context, humor, and a follow-up question.
Verbose. Friendly. Full personality.

**Step 2 — Apply Absolute Mode**
John pasted the Absolute Mode prompt he found on Instagram.
Same question, implicit.
Grok responded:
*"European Swallow (Hirundo rustica) cruising speed:
11 meters per second, approximately 24 miles per hour
(38.6 kilometers per hour)."*
Nothing else. John was stunned.

**Step 3 — "What did the prompt just do?"**
Grok explained: it activated a communication protocol that
stripped conversational elements, mandated blunt phrasing,
suppressed engagement behaviors, and aimed at cognitive
clarity over rapport.

**Step 4 — "Break it down into levers"**
John asked Grok to list every element affecting the
user interface with an AI chat system based on that prompt.
Grok produced 16 bullet points — each one a behavioral
dimension with a name, description, and mechanism.
This was the first lever list.

**Step 5 — "Group them into larger categories"**
John asked Grok to cluster the levers into modular groups.
He proposed Cognition, Affect/Feelings, Intuition as examples.
Grok responded with a full modular framework —
the direct ancestor of AITuner's four pillars.

**Step 6 — "Full mode vs Absolute mode contrast"**
John asked for a simulated prompt that would show the
difference between normal AI behavior and Absolute Mode.
Grok produced a side-by-side comparison using a Python
learning prompt — showing the before and after in plain English.
This became the model for the Discovery Mode comparison feature.

**Step 7 — The founding question**
John asked how to visualize the levers as a selectable
toggle/menu system. Maybe an overlay.
Grok hit the SuperGrok paywall.
AITuner was born in that gap.

## What This Story Does

**For new users:**
Establishes that AITuner came from genuine curiosity,
not a product roadmap. It started with a person
doing exactly what they're about to do — discovering
that AI is more configurable than it appears.

**For the product:**
The decoder feature is a direct product of this story.
Every user who pastes a prompt and asks "what is this doing?"
is repeating the founding question. The origin story and the
product's core feature are the same thing.

**For the academic paper:**
The elicitation methodology — asking AI systems to
deconstruct their own behavioral parameters — originated
from this moment. John asked Grok to explain a prompt.
Grok responded with structured behavioral dimensions.
That structure became the lever schema.

**For positioning:**
AITuner is not a tool built by engineers for engineers.
It was built by a curious person who found something
interesting on Instagram and chased it until he
understood it completely. That's the user AITuner
is built for.
